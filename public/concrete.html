Why Quality Matters

Why Does Concrete Crack
and Fail?

Most concrete failures aren't about the concrete itself — they're about the shortcuts taken before the pour. Cheap contractors skip the prep, rush the forms, and skip reinforcement. You end up paying twice.

Poor Base Prep & No Compaction
Soft or unstable sub-base is the #1 cause of cracking and settlement. If the ground underneath shifts, the concrete above cracks — guaranteed.
Skipping Reinforcement
Unreinforced concrete handles compression well but fails under tensile stress. Rebar or wire mesh isn't optional on driveways and slabs — it's what holds things together over time.
Wrong Mix or Rushed Cure
Adding too much water to the mix weakens it significantly. Not allowing proper cure time leads to surface scaling and premature deterioration — especially in Oklahoma's heat.
Sloppy Forming & Drainage Ignored
Poorly set forms mean uneven thickness. No drainage plan means water pools and works its way underneath — the freeze-thaw cycle in Oklahoma winters does the rest.
The Sparrow Standard

Proper Prep. Proper Reinforcement. No Shortcuts.

We grade, compact, and inspect the base before we set a single form
We use rebar or wire mesh on every driveway and structural slab — not as an upsell, as a standard
We use proper mix designs and never dilute on site to speed up the pour
We plan drainage into every job — so water goes where it's supposed to go, not under your slab
We set forms clean and pull them clean — your edges will look right, not rough and ragged
The result?
Concrete that lasts 20–30 years without the cracking, heaving, or crumbling you see on cheaply done jobs. The upfront cost is similar — the long-term difference is enormous.

Concrete FAQ

Common Concrete Questions

A standard concrete driveway in the OKC area typically runs $8–$12 per square foot for a new pour, and $10–$18 per square foot for demo-and-replace. Final pricing depends on thickness, reinforcement, site access, demolition of old concrete, finish type, and site conditions. A typical 2-car driveway (~800 sq ft) runs $6,400–$9,600+ depending on scope. We'll give you a firm written quote after seeing the job — no guesswork.
Standard residential driveways should be poured at 4 inches minimum — 5 to 6 inches if the driveway will bear heavier vehicles like RVs or trucks. Patios are typically 4 inches. Foundations and building slabs are usually 4–6 inches depending on load requirements. Going thinner to save money is one of the most common shortcuts — and one of the most common causes of early cracking and failure.
Yes — we handle full tear-out, demo, and haul-away of existing concrete as part of our replacement process. Demo costs are factored into your quote. After demolition, we re-grade and compact the base properly before pouring fresh concrete — this step is critical and many cheap contractors skip it entirely. We don't.
Properly installed concrete — with correct base prep, appropriate thickness, and reinforcement — should last 20 to 30 years or more in Oklahoma's climate. Concrete that was poorly prepared, under-reinforced, or poured with too much water can show significant cracking within 5–10 years. The difference is entirely in how the job is done.
Most residential concrete jobs — a driveway, patio, or set of sidewalks — are completed in 1–2 days. The pour itself often takes a few hours; the bulk of the time is in proper prep (grading, compacting, forming) and the curing period. Concrete should not be driven on for at least 3–7 days after pouring. Large commercial jobs are staged across multiple days. We'll give you a realistic timeline with your estimate.
