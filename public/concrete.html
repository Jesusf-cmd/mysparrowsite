Concrete FAQ

Common Concrete Questions

Answers from a licensed Oklahoma concrete contractor.

Most residential driveways in OKC cost between $6–$12 per square foot installed, depending on size, thickness, site conditions, and finish type. We provide free on-site estimates so you get an accurate price.
Standard residential driveways should be 4 inches thick minimum. For heavy vehicles or commercial applications, we pour at 5–6 inches with additional reinforcement for long-term durability.
We use 3,000–4,000 PSI concrete for residential and commercial projects in Oklahoma. This high-strength mix is rated for freeze-thaw cycles and heavy vehicle loads common in OKC.
Rebar provides superior structural strength and is ideal for driveways and heavy-load areas. Wire mesh works well for patios and sidewalks. We recommend the right reinforcement based on your specific project and soil conditions.
Concrete reaches about 70% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days. You can typically walk on it after 24–48 hours and drive on a driveway after 7 days. We provide specific curing instructions for every project.
Yes — full tear-out and haul-away is included in all replacement projects. We handle demolition, removal, disposal, and complete site cleanup before the new pour.
Hairline cracks can occur naturally, but proper base prep, reinforcement, control joints, and curing dramatically reduce cracking. Our methods are specifically designed for Oklahoma's clay soil and temperature extremes.
Yes. We offer stamped, brushed, and smooth finish options for patios and decorative concrete. Stamped concrete can mimic the look of stone, brick, or slate at a fraction of the cost.
Base prep is the most critical step in any concrete project. In Oklahoma, clay soil expands and contracts with moisture — a proper 4–6 inch compacted gravel base prevents settling, shifting, and premature cracking.
Permit requirements vary by city and project scope. In Oklahoma City and surrounding areas, driveways and larger projects typically require a permit. We can help guide you through the permitting process.
A properly installed concrete driveway can last 25–50 years or more. The key factors are correct thickness, quality base prep, reinforcement, and proper drainage — all of which we guarantee on every project.
Oklahoma has expansive clay soil that swells when wet and shrinks when dry. Without proper base preparation and drainage, this movement causes concrete to crack, heave, and settle. Our methods are specifically designed for these conditions.
We offer brushed (broom), smooth, stamped, and exposed aggregate finishes. Brushed is the most popular for driveways, while stamped and smooth finishes are common for patios and decorative applications.
Most residential pours are completed in 1–2 days for the pour itself. Including tear-out, base prep, and finishing, a typical driveway project takes 3–5 days total. Curing is 7 days before light use.
