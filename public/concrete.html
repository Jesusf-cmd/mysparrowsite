Why Quality Matters

Why Does Concrete Crack and Fail?

Most concrete failures aren't about the concrete itself — they're about the shortcuts taken before the pour. Cheap contractors skip the prep, rush the forms, and skip reinforcement. You end up paying twice.

✕
Poor Base Prep & No Compaction
Soft or unstable sub-base is the #1 cause of cracking and settlement. If the ground underneath shifts, the concrete above cracks — guaranteed.
✕
Skipping Reinforcement
Unreinforced concrete handles compression well but fails under tensile stress. Rebar or wire mesh isn't optional on driveways and slabs.
✕
Wrong Mix or Rushed Cure
Adding too much water to the mix weakens it significantly. Not allowing proper cure time leads to surface scaling and premature deterioration.
✕
Sloppy Forming & Drainage Ignored
Poorly set forms mean uneven thickness. No drainage plan means water pools and works its way underneath.
The Sparrow Standard

Proper Prep. Proper Reinforcement. No Shortcuts.

We grade, compact, and inspect the base before we set a single form
We use rebar or wire mesh on every driveway and structural slab — not as an upsell, as a standard

What Affects Your Final Price

Every concrete job is different. Here's what we look at when building your quote:
Thickness — standard residential driveways are 4", commercial and heavy-load areas may need 6" or more
Reinforcement — wire mesh or rebar adds cost but dramatically extends lifespan
Demolition — tearing out existing concrete adds labor and haul-away cost
Site access — tight spaces or slope can affect how we stage equipment
Finish type — broom, smooth, exposed aggregate, or stamped affect labor time
Site conditions — soft soil may require additional base material or compaction work
